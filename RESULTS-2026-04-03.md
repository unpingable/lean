### Static topology (TaxonomyGraph.lean)

**Terminal set is exactly {Δg, Δa, Δx, Δh}.**
Proved by exhaustive case analysis. Every other node has at least one outgoing pipeline edge.

**Three terminal families, not one universal sink.**

| Family | Terminals | Reached by |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| Gain/actuation | Δg, Δa | Δs, Δm (exclusively); Δn, Δo, Δb, Δp, Δr (among others) |
| Scale | Δx | Δk (exclusively) |

**The coupling family {Δk, Δx} is graph-isolated.**
`notCoupling` (complement of {Δk, Δx}) is forward-closed. No pipeline path crosses from the rest of the graph into the coupling family.

**Closure map (all cells machine-verified):**

### Dynamic persistence (PersistenceModel.lean)

**Δc → Δh under persistence**, modeled as a state machine with:
- States: `aligned`, `detachedShort`, `detachedWarn`, `hysteretic`
- Variables: `detachAge` (episode commit count), `rollbackCapacity` (consumable budget)
- Semantics: only detached commits burn capacity; idle detachment does not

**Six invariants proved:**

1. **Capacity never increases** — rollback budget is monotone non-increasing
2. **Idle preserves capacity** — with idleBurn=0, idle steps don't degrade
3. **Hysteretic is absorbing** — no event or sequence can leave it
4. **Reattach from hysteretic fails** — internal return unavailable
5. **Hysteresis without DETACHED_WARN** — a trace reaches hysteretic from aligned without ever entering detachedWarn (HEADLINE)
6. **DETACHED_WARN requires prolonged episode** — entering it requires commit count ≥ τ; confirms diagnostic, not causal role

## What was killed

### Dead slogan 1: "Δh is the universal sink"

- **Old claim:** All failure modes eventually flow to Δh through the pipeline.
- **Lean says:** FALSE as static pipeline topology. Δs and Δk cannot reach Δh through any pipeline path. The signal family dead-ends at Δg/Δa. The coupling family dead-ends at Δx.
- **Replacement:** "Static topology yields multiple terminal families; any universalization of Δh must be stated as a temporal hypothesis, not a graph fact."

### Dead slogan 2: "Prolonged detachment leads to reset failure"

- **Old claim:** Sustained contiguous detachment is what produces hysteresis.
- **Lean says:** FALSE as necessary condition. Hysteresis can occur without ever entering `detachedWarn`. Repeated short episodes with cumulative capacity burn suffice.
- **Replacement:** "Reset failure is driven by cumulative rollback depletion under detached commits; prolonged contiguous detachment is sufficient but not necessary."

### Corollary: Episode recoverability ≠ lifetime recoverability

The Python model showed (and Lean's invariant 1 + 5 formalize) that a system can recover from every individual detachment episode while still becoming irrecoverable across episodes. Each "successful" recovery leaves a scar. Rollback capacity does not regenerate.

## What the informal prose was doing wrong

The theory was conflating:

1. **Pipeline reachability** (static graph property) with **temporal attractor dynamics** (dynamic state property) — compressed into "universal sink"
2. **Contiguous duration** with **cumulative commitment** — compressed into "long enough"
3. **Episode outcome** with **lifetime trajectory** — compressed into "recoverable"

All three conflations made the theory sound stronger than it was. The formalizations force the distinctions.
